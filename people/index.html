People

Dr. Sami Domisch is a Leibniz-Junior Research Group Leader and is interested in freshwater biodiversity and biogeography across spatial scales, and in developing tools in spatial freshwater biodiversity science. Dr. Jaime Garcia Marquez is a GIS Data Analyst with a strong emphasis in open-source methods & tools for analysing large biodiversity data sets using the Linux command-line and the High Performance Computing cluster. Afroditi Grigoropoulou is a PhD student focusing on global aquatic insect biodiversity, with an emphasis on patterns of aquatic insect diversity across multiple spatial scales.

Sami Domisch is a Leibniz Junior Research Group Leader at the Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). Sami is interested in freshwater biodiversity with an emphasis on spatial biodiversity patterns, macroecology, and developing tools to assess freshwater biodiversity across large spatial scales. The group uses a wide array of open-source tools for spatial biodiversity informatics such as R, GRASS, GDAL/OGR, pktools, OpenForis within the Linux environment. Career since 2019 - Leibniz-Junior Research Group Leader at Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries 2016-2019 - Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (Postdoc) 2013-2016 - Yale University, USA (Postdoctoral associate and DFG postdoctoral fellow) 2012-2013 - Biodiversity and Climate Research Centre (BiK-F), Frankfurt, Germany (Postdoc) Funding and awards Alexander von Humboldt Foundation: Co-host for Dr.

Afroditi Grigoropoulou is a PhD student at the Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). She is working on global patterns of freshwater macroinvertebrate diversity across multiple spatial scales. Career since 2020 - PhD student at Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries 2018-2019 - Department of Evolutionary Biology, Ecology and Environmental Science, University of Barcelona (MSc) 2013-2017 - Faculty of Biology, University of Athens (BSc)

Annika Brunner is a master student at Freie Universität Berlin and writes her master thesis at the Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). Annika is integrating Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs) into freshwater biodiversity projections in South America.

Jaime Garcia Marquez is a GIS and Data Analyst at the Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). Marlene Üblacker is a PhD student at the Leibniz-Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). She is working on a high-resolution freshwater habitat characterization and is conducting analyses across spatial scales to highlight distinct patterns of aquatic insects and potential gaps in the global network of protected areas.
