People

Meet the team members

Last updated on Sep 14, 2020

Dr. Sami Domisch is a Leibniz-Junior Research Group Leader and is interested in freshwater biodiversity and biogeography across spatial scales, and in developing tools in spatial freshwater biodiversity science.

Dr. Jaime Garcia Marquez is a GIS Data Analyst with a strong emphasis in open-source methods & tools for analysing large biodiversity data sets using the Linux command-line and the High Performance Computing cluster.

Afroditi Grigoropoulou is a PhD student focusing on global aquatic insect biodiversity, with an emphasis on patterns of aquatic insect diversity across multiple spatial scales.

Maria Üblacker is a PhD student working on a high-resolution freshwater habitat characterization across spatial scales, to highlight patterns and potential gaps in the global network of protected areas.

Martin Friedrichs-Manthay is a PhD student working on fish distribution models, assessing scale-dependencies and model uncertainties for conservation (funded by the GLANCE project and co-supervised by Sami)

Annika Brunner is a master Student at Freie Universität Berlin and writes her master thesis at IGB. Annika integrates Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs) into freshwater biodiversity projections.

Thomas Tomiczek is a master student from the Humboldt University working on his master thesis at the IGB. His thesis addresses integrating freshwater biodiversity in large scale conservation planning in Colombia.

Lydia Nass is a master student from the Freie Universität Berlin. She is doing an intership at the IGB, focusing on open-source geocomputational and species distribution modelling methods.

Xiling Deng is a visiting PhD student from the Senckenberg Research Institute and Natural History Museum. She is working on the phylogeography of caddiesflies along elevational gradients.
